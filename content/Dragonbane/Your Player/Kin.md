# Races of Karameikos — Expanded Names

## Humans

### Traladarans
![[z_Assets/Traldaran Family.png| right lp | 300]]Traladaran names have a strong **Slavic and Balkan** flavor. Family names often use patronymic endings like **-ovich** (son of) or **-ovna** (daughter of), or descriptive village-based names.  

- **Male Names:** Boris, Dmitri, Fyodor, Grigory, Ilya, Ivan, Kiril, Mikhail, Pyotr, Sergei, Stefan, Yakov, Yuri, Zoran, Vasil, Oleg.  
- **Female Names:** Anya, Darya, Ecatrina, Ilyana, Irena, Katarina, Magda, Misha, Petra, Sula, Zandra, Milena, Vesna, Tatiana, Yelena.  
- **Family Names:** Dragomirov, Volkov, Petrescu, Markov, Vasiliev, Horescu, Drazhkov, Karamov, Novak, Ilyanovich.  
### Mixed
Children of Traladarans and Thyatians often blend traditions. Many have a **Traladaran first name** with a **Thyatian family name**, or vice versa.  

- **Examples:** Ivan Vorloi, Claudia Dragomirov, Pyotr Tullius, Magda Aurelia.  
### Thyatians
![[z_Assets/Thyatian Noble.png| right lp | 300]]Thyatian names echo **Roman, Byzantine, and Mediterranean** styles. Nobles often favor long, sonorous names; commoners lean on simpler classical roots.  

- **Male Names:** Alexander, Valerius, Claudius, Marcus, Lucius, Tiberius, Maximus, Dorian, Julian, Antonius, Cassian, Varis, Adrian.  
- **Female Names:** Valeria, Livia, Julia, Claudia, Anastasia, Flavia, Marcella, Helena, Domina, Aelia, Octavia, Aurelia, Marina.  
- **Family Names:** Karameikos, Penhaligon, Torion, Vorloi, Demetrios, Tullius, Varro, Corvus, Aurelian, Korrigan.

## Halflings
![[z_Assets/Halfling.png| right lp | 300]]Halfling names often sound **rustic and whimsical**, reflecting their love of hearth and story. In Karameikos, most halflings trace roots to the **Five Shires**, but borrow local names too.  

- **Male Names:** Cottar, Perrywick, Samno, Dandel, Roderic, Tobin, Harl, Bramble, Merro, Fennel, Oswin.  
- **Female Names:** Theoline, Brina, Clover, Daisy, Amaran, Poppy, Miri, Tansy, Jessel, Willow, Rowan.  
- **Family Names:** Greenhill, Brambletoe, Underbough, Tealeaf, Thornapple, Goodbarrel, Proudfoot, Longriver.  

---

## Dwarves
![[z_Assets/Dwarf.png| right lp | 300]]Dwarves use strong, **hard-edged names** rooted in clan and craft. The **Stronghollow clan** of Highforge is most famous. Family names often include elements like **-rock, -hammer, -forge, -stone**.  

- **Male Names:** Urd, Fermer, Traut, Balrik, Moradin, Dain, Brogar, Korrun, Stenvar, Thrain, Durgan.  
- **Female Names:** Hilda, Brynja, Greta, Sigrid, Tovra, Helga, Anika, Freydis, Bruni, Thora, Ysolde.  
- **Family Names:** Stronghollow, Ironfist, Stonebeard, Rockdelver, Hammerthane, Goldvein, Blackanvil.  

---

## Elves
The **Callarii** elves favor flowing, melodic names, often with references to stars, rivers, or the forest. Family/clan names are tied to **nature imagery** (leaves, silver, dawn).  

- **Male Names:** Arasin, Galvander, Erwilnor, Thalion, Caelir, Sylvaris, Tyrindel, Faelir, Lorandel, Eryndor.  
- **Female Names:** Illyriana, Tyrindelia, Andremone, Selindra, Calithra, Yavanna, Ellarian, Serelith, Aranya, Vaelora.  
- **Family Names/Clans:** Callarii, Moonbrook, Silverleaf, Starbough, Greenbranch, Dawnwhisper, Rivensong.  

The **Vyalia** elves (rarer in Karameikos) lean toward **hushed, enigmatic names** with sharper consonants.

## Gnomes (#HomebrewDetail)
Gnomes of Highforge delight in bright, curious-sounding names, often with double consonants and playful cadences. Family names often reference crafts or clever inventions.  

- **Male Names:** Brindel, Dornic, Filbor, Quibble, Rannick, Tovik, Mernan, Wizzle, Gadrin.  
- **Female Names:** Nessa, Felbara, Brilla, Torvia, Lissel, Ormella, Pippa, Cendra, Ysolma.  
- **Family Names:** Cogspinner, Ironbuckle, Gemcutter, Bronzewheel, Oakengear, Quillwhistle, Stonelamp.
